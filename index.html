Orientation Tracking & Panoramic Image Construction

We proposed a framwork for creating robust panoramas, overcoming sensor errors and motion issues. It recalibrates IMU data using static detectors for precise bias and orientation, employs glitch-aware descent for stable orientation tracking, and uses equidistant projection for efficient image stitching.

Lidar-SLAM

Our project focuses on pose estimation and map construction for a differential drive robot, enhancing accuracy with an advanced Iterative Closest Point (ICP) algorithm that integrates downsampling, multi-resolution techniques, and KD-trees. We utilize a motion model and ICP scan matching for the front-end, while the back-end employs factor graphs and loop closure to mitigate drift and boost trajectory reliability. Extensive tests across diverse indoor environments have confirmed the robustness of our system.

Paper
Visual-Inertial SLAM via Extended Kalman Filter

We developed a sophisticated Extended Kalman Filter (EKF)-based framework for pose estimation and mapping in GPS-denied scenarios. Our approach utilizes SE(3) kinematics and IMU data for the predict step, and leverages a stereo-camera observation model for landmark-based mapping during the update step.
